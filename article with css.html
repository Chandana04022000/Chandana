SUNITHA WILLIAMS

Sunita Williams is an American astronaut and United States Navy officer of Indian-Slovenian descent. She is known for holding records related to spaceflight, particularly for her time aboard the International Space Station (ISS).

basic information

  • Full Name: Sunita Lyn Williams
  • Born: September 19, 1965 (Age: 59 as of 2024)
  • Birthplace: Euclid, Ohio, USA
  • Nationality: American
  • Occupation: Astronaut, Test Pilot, Navy Officer
  • Space Agency: NASA
  • Education: Bachelor’s degree in Physical Science from the U.S. Naval Academy (1987) Master’s degree in Engineering Management from the Florida Institute of Technology (1995)

Space Missions

Expedition 14/15 (December 9, 2006 – June 22, 2007)

Spent 195 days in space Conducted four spacewalks, totaling over 29 hours Set a record for the longest spaceflight by a woman at the time

Expedition 32/33 (July 15, 2012 – November 18, 2012)

Served as commander of the ISS (second woman to do so) Conducted three more spacewalks, bringing her total to 50+ hours Held the record for most spacewalking time by a female astronaut (until 2017)

Upcoming Mission

Sunita Williams is set to fly aboard Boeing’s Starliner spacecraft for its first crewed test flight to the ISS, currently expected in 2024.

Achievements & Records

  • Second Indian-origin woman in space after Kalpana Chawla.
  • First woman to complete the Boston Marathon from space (April 16, 2007).
  • Total space time: Over 321 days across her missions.
  • Rank in the U.S. Navy: Captain.

Personal Life & Interests

  • Loves swimming, running, biking, and flying.
  • Owns a pet dog named "Gorby."
  • Inspired by her father, an Indian immigrant and medical professional.
